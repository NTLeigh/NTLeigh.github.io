Spectroelectrochemical Detection of Dopamine and Serotonin

  • In this proof-of-concept study, a label-free colorimetric detection method of neurotransmitters was proposed by observing the electrodeâ€™s localized surface plasmon resonance (LSPR) responses during the electrochemical process. Nanostructured working electrodes, electrochemically decorated with gold nanoparticles (AuNPs), were utilized as probes for such optical- chemical process. Cyclic voltammetry (CV) was performed using such probe, and to detect the oxidation and recovery of neurotransmitters, such as dopamine and serotonin, alone with ascorbic acid as the interfering substance. Colorimetric changes was observed during the electrochemical process, simultaneously accompanied by the currents at the characteristic oxidation potentials. Complemented by the high sensitivity of LSPR detection and the specificity of electrochemistry, the dual detection method could serve as a better alternative platform.

(d) Cyclic voltammogram of different films in redox pair of K3Fe(CN)6 and K4Fe(CN)6. Results indicated that the deposited AuNPs could dramatically enhance the conductivity of the sensing device.
